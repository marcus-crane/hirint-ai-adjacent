# About the Team

The Robot Services team is responsible for maintaining and improving the health of our deployed robotic fleet. As robots operate in customer homes and real-world environments, this team diagnoses failures, performs repairs, and partners closely with Engineering to improve reliability, serviceability, and overall fleet performance.

# Your Charter

As a Robot Service Technician, you will be responsible for diagnosing, repairing, testing, and validating humanoid robots and their subsystems. Your work will directly impact fleet uptime, product reliability, and the speed at which engineering teams can learn from real-world robot performance.

# Key Outcomes

* Complete high-quality repairs and maintenance activities that return robots to service safely and efficiently.
* Diagnose mechanical, electrical, and system-level failures while documenting root causes and corrective actions.
* Execute validation and test procedures to ensure repaired robots meet operational and performance requirements.
* Maintain accurate service records that support fleet reliability analysis and continuous product improvement.
* Identify recurring failure trends and provide actionable feedback to Engineering and Operations teams.

# Key Competencies

* Strong electromechanical troubleshooting and repair skills.
* High attention to detail and craftsmanship when working on precision assemblies.
* Ability to follow structured processes while maintaining a strong sense of ownership and accountability.
* Clear communicator who can document technical findings and collaborate across teams.
* Comfortable working in a fast-paced environment where priorities and challenges evolve quickly.

# Minimum Requirements

* 2+ years of experience in robotics, manufacturing, aerospace, automotive, electronics, or another hands-on technical field.
* Experience assembling, maintaining, or repairing electromechanical systems.
* Ability to read and interpret technical drawings, schematics, and work instructions.
* Experience using measurement and diagnostic tools such as calipers, multimeters, torque tools, or similar equipment.
* Ability to work safely around electrical systems, lift components, and perform hands-on work for extended periods.

# Preferred Skills

* Experience working on robotic systems, automation equipment, or complex electromechanical products.
* Experience with wiring harness repair, precision soldering, crimping, or connector assembly.
* Familiarity with command-line tools, hardware diagnostics, or embedded system troubleshooting.
