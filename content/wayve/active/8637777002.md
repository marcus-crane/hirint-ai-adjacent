## **The role**

As a Senior Engineering Technician, you’ll play a pivotal role in building and scaling the engineering operations that enable Wayve’s autonomous vehicle platform. You’ll lead a small team of Engineering Technicians while remaining deeply hands-on, partnering closely with Electrical, Mechanical, Systems and Software Engineers to rapidly prototype, integrate, troubleshoot and validate cutting-edge hardware. This is an opportunity to bring proven technician expertise into a fast-paced robotics environment, establishing best practices while helping the team move quickly without compromising quality.

**Key responsibilities:**

* Lead, mentor and develop a team of Engineering Technicians.
* Own day-to-day lab operations, hardware bring-up, integration and vehicle support activities.
* Execute and oversee electrical, mechanical and system-level builds, modifications and repairs.
* Partner with engineering teams to diagnose complex hardware and integration issues across development programs.
* Develop and improve technician workflows, lab processes and documentation to increase efficiency and quality.
* Support prototype builds, validation activities and engineering change implementation.
* Drive a culture of safety, craftsmanship and continuous improvement within the technician team.

## About you

In order to set you up for success as a Senior Engineering Technician at Wayve, we’re looking for the following skills and experience.

Essential

* 5+ years of experience as an Engineering Technician or Engineering Technician Lead supporting complex hardware products.
* Previous experience leading or mentoring technicians while remaining an active technical contributor.
* Strong hands-on experience with electrical and mechanical assembly, integration, debugging and rework.
* Proven ability to troubleshoot multidisciplinary hardware issues independently in fast-paced development environments.
* Experience supporting prototype and low-volume hardware builds through rapid design iterations.
* Comfortable interpreting engineering drawings, schematics, wiring diagrams and assembly documentation.
* Experience working closely with Electrical, Mechanical and Systems Engineering teams to resolve integration challenges.
* A strong understanding of lab equipment, test instrumentation and safe working practices.
* Demonstrated ability to establish technician best practices, improve workflows and raise execution quality.
* Excellent organisational skills with the ability to prioritise competing tasks across multiple engineering programs.
* Strong communication skills and the confidence to collaborate across engineering disciplines.

Desirable

* Experience supporting autonomous vehicles, robotics, aerospace, automotive or other complex electromechanical systems.
* Familiarity with vehicle electrical systems, wire harness fabrication and integration.
* Experience managing engineering labs, inventory and calibration processes.
* Experience implementing lean manufacturing or continuous improvement methodologies.
* Comfortable using Linux, scripting tools or basic software utilities to support engineering workflows.
* Experience working with PLM, ERP or engineering change management systems.
* Associate’s or Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering Technology, Mechanical Engineering Technology or equivalent practical experience.
