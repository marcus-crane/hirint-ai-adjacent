## The role

The Fleet Operations & Field Engineering Lead is the senior on-site leader responsible for building, leading, and scaling Wayve’s on-road operations in Israel.  
Based in Herzliya and reporting to the Head of Field Engineering, this role owns the full operational stack in-region, including fleet uptime, ADAS testing delivery, regulatory compliance, and overall operational performance.  
  
This is a hands-on leadership role, combining deep technical expertise with operational ownership. You will act as the technical authority in-region, leading fault triage across vehicle hardware, software, sensors, and ADAS systems, while also operating as a Vehicle Safety Operator.  
  
You will start with a lean operating model and build the foundations for scalable, safe, and high-performing operations. This includes establishing processes, driving performance against KPIs, and representing Wayve locally with regulators and stakeholders.  
  
This is a unique opportunity to build operations from first principles in a new region while contributing to Wayve’s global 24/7 follow-the-sun fleet operations model.

**Key responsibilities:**

* Lead hands-on fault triage and resolution across vehicle hardware, software, sensors, and compute systems.
* Act as a certified Vehicle Safety Operator, delivering structured on-road ADAS testing.
* Deliver high-quality testing outputs, including accurate reporting, QA, and auditability.
* Ensure fleet uptime, readiness, and utilisation targets are consistently met.
* Drive operational performance through structured planning, execution, and KPI tracking.
* Establish and enforce safe, repeatable processes for triage, repair, testing, and compliance.
* Maintain a 5-minute SLA response model for vehicle issues, both locally and globally.
* Build, manage, and develop a small high-performing team.
* Recruit, onboard, and scale the Israel Fleet Operations team as needed.
* Act as the local operational and regulatory representative for Wayve in Israel.
* Coordinate with cross-functional teams including Reliability Engineering, SRE, Safety, Legal, and global Fleet Ops.
* Contribute to global handovers to enable 24/7 fleet coverage.
* Embed and uphold a strong safety-first culture across all operations.
* Ensure compliance with regulatory, legal, and safety requirements without compromising development velocity.
* Manage site setup, infrastructure readiness, and operational compliance.
* Represent Wayve in external engagements, including regulators, partners, and executive demos.
* Provide structured updates, risk visibility, and insights to senior leadership.
* Participate in an on-call rotation to support rapid fleet response.

## About you

You are a highly capable operator-leader who thrives in complex, safety-critical environments. You combine strong technical problem-solving with the ability to build and scale operations from the ground up. You are comfortable being both hands-on and strategic, able to diagnose vehicle issues one moment and make high-level operational decisions the next.   
  
You bring structure to ambiguity, operate effectively under pressure, and understand how to balance speed, safety, compliance, and performance.   
  
You are a strong communicator and stakeholder manager, able to represent Wayve both internally and externally while building trust across teams and partners.

**Essential:**

* Strong hands-on experience in automotive, mobility, robotics, aviation, defence, or other safety-critical environments.
* Experience diagnosing and resolving vehicle hardware and software issues.
* Experience with ADAS or autonomous vehicle testing.
* Proven experience leading small teams while remaining technically hands-on.
* Experience operating in regulated environments.
* Ability to build structured processes and deliver against strict SLAs.
* Strong stakeholder management and communication skills.
* Demonstrated ability to balance hands-on execution with strategic leadership.
* Experience launching or scaling new operational sites.
* Fluent in Hebrew and English.
* Hold a full Israeli Driving Licence.

**Desirable:**

* Fleet importation, homologation, or regulatory liaison experience.
* Experience with vehicle systems integration, diagnostics, or networked vehicle platforms.
* EV charging infrastructure or site setup experience.
* Experience in ADAS/AV development or testing environments.
* Experience operating in zero-shot or generalisation testing contexts**.**
* Professional manager qualification.

**Additional Requirements:**

* Onsite in Herzliya, Israel - Sunday to Thursday.
* Participation in an on-call rotation is required.
* Occasional international travel may be required to support other sites or share best practice.
* Training and onboarding will take place in London and the US.
