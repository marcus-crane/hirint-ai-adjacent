## **Who we are**

Helsing is a defence AI company. Our mission is to protect our democracies. We aim to achieve technological leadership, so that open societies can continue to make sovereign decisions and control their ethical standards. 

As democracies, we believe we have a special responsibility to be thoughtful about the development and deployment of powerful technologies like AI. We take this responsibility seriously. 

We are an ambitious and committed team of engineers, AI specialists and customer-facing programme managers. We are looking for mission-driven people to join our European teams – and apply their skills to solve the most complex and impactful problems. We embrace an open and transparent culture that welcomes healthy debates on the use of technology in defence, its benefits, and its ethical implications.

## **The role**

As a Mission Systems Architect at Helsing, you will define and drive the functional, logical and physical architectures underpinning our software-defined mission system for the future combat air domain. Working at the intersection of hardware, software and systems engineering, you will guide partner organisations developing mission computer hardware, ensure the right integration infrastructure is in place, and provide technical direction to the software team. Your work will shape how autonomous mission systems are built, from inception through to flight test.

## **The day-to-day**

* Define and own the mission system architecture, establishing the technical foundation that enables safe, certified and autonomous capability from requirements through to qualification
* Guide and govern partner organisations developing mission computer hardware, ensuring alignment with integration requirements, avionics communications architecture and real-time performance constraints
* Lead the systems engineering lifecycle across the full development programme, driving technical deliverables, compliance documentation and adherence to relevant aviation and military standards
* Act as the primary technical interface between the mission systems and software teams, translating architectural intent into actionable guidance that accelerates software development against the product vision
* Report programme status and technical changes to Chief Engineering and Programme Management, providing clear, evidence-based insight that supports timely decision-making

## **You should apply if you**

* Have built and delivered airborne mission system architectures for military applications, with a clear vision for how next-generation, software-defined systems should be designed to support autonomous use cases
* Possess deep knowledge of Integrated Modular Avionics platforms, including systems engineering, certification, and integration of airborne mission computers
* Understand mission system sensor domains, including communications, optics, radar and self-defence systems, and can translate that understanding into robust architectural decisions
* Are fluent in military open standards for avionics (such as MOSA, SOSA, FACE and Pyramid) and their application to hardware development
* Have hands-on experience applying DO-178C, DO-254 and DO-297 to complex hardware and software development, and can communicate technical outcomes clearly to both engineering teams and senior stakeholders

**Note: We operate in an industry where women, as well as other minority groups, are systematically* *under-represented. We encourage you to apply even if you don’t meet all the listed qualifications; ability and impact cannot be summarised in a few bullet points.**
