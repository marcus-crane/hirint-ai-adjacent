## **Who we are**

Helsing develops artificial intelligence-enabled capabilities to protect and defend democracies. We are growing our US operations, cultivating an ambitious and committed team of mission-driven professionals to apply their skills to solve challenging problems.

## **The role**

Are you ready to share the financial future of a company on the forefront of national security innovation? As the Director of Finance for Helsing’s US Business, you will serve as a strategic architect during a pivotal growth phase – building scalable financial infrastructure, driving data-informed decisions, and partnering directly with executive leadership and our European team to build US and global processes and organization. This is more than spreadsheets and forecasts; it’s an opportunity to make a tangible impact in the Defense Tech space while navigating the unique complexities of operating under a Special Security Agreement.  If you thrive in fast-paced, high-stakes environments where your work directly enables breakthrough technologies for the warfighter, we want to hear from you.

## **The day-to-day**

* Lead all financial planning, budgeting, forecasting, and cash flow management to support rapid scaling and strategic objectives for the US Subsidiary of Helsing
* Work with the Global Finance team to design scalable processes the foster connectivity and alignment between geographies
* Develop and present financial analyses, KPIs, and board-level reporting that drive executive decision-making and investor confidence
* Ensure full compliance with SSA governance requirements, maintaining appropriate separation protocols while optimizing financial operations across the organization
* Lead the identification and capture of federal, state, and local incentives tied to manufacturing CAPEX and OPEX—including accelerated depreciation, R&D credits, and NOL strategies
* Partner with technical and procurement teams to design cost-efficient U.S. supply chain, intercompany, and contracting structures that satisfy federal and/or state procurement requirements, while developing strategies to recover or minimize tariff exposure on imported goods and materials
* Serve as the financial lead for U.S. manufacturing stand-up, establishing sound CAPEX/OPEX, procurement, and intercompany frameworks from inception
* Build, mentor, and scale a high-performing finance team capable of supporting our growth trajectory
* Partner cross-functionally with Operations, Contracts, and Program Management to ensure financial alignment on government and commercial contracts
* Manage relationships with external auditors, tax advisors, and banking partners; oversee annual audits and regulatory filings
* Support M&A activities including due diligence, financial modeling, integration planning, and post-acquisition optimization

## **You should apply if you**

* You have 10+ years of progressive finance experience, with at least 3 years in a leadership role
* You have built processes and systems from scratch
* You've operated within the Defense, Aerospace, or National Security technology sector and understand the nuances of government contracting
* Prior experience reporting to a Board of Directors or foreign parent company stakeholders under governance constraints
* You've built or scaled finance functions at a startup or high-growth company and know how to balance scrappiness with rigor
* You possess strong command of financial modeling, scenario planning, and capital allocation strategies
* You communicate complex financial concepts with clarity and influence to technical and non-technical stakeholders alike
* You're energized by ambiguity, thrive under pressure, and are motivated by mission-driven work

Note: We encourage you to apply even if you don’t meet all the listed qualifications; ability and impact cannot be summarized in a few bullet points. 

## **Nice to Have**

* CPA, CFA, or MBA from a top-tier program

* Background working with cleared facilities, FOCI mitigation, and DSS/DCSA interactions
* Proficiency with ERP systems (NetSuite, Deltek Costpoint, or similar) and FP&A tools
* You have direct experience working under a Special Security Agreement (SSA) or Proxy Agreement structure with a foreign-owned parent company
