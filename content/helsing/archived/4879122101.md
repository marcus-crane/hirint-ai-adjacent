## **The role**

As Ground Test Engineer, you will own the systematic de-risking of Helsing's ground-side systems before flight, shifting failure discovery from the expensive flight-test phase into the controlled laboratory environment. Working across Ground Control Stations, launch systems and data links, you will build and maintain the test infrastructure, regression libraries and HIL/SIL environments that serve as the gatekeeping layer between bench and airspace. Your work directly reduces programme cost, prevents in-flight failures and provides the documented technical evidence required for airworthiness compliance.

## The day-to-day

* Execute high-repetition burn-in tests on all new firmware and hardware configurations for the Ground Control Station and data links, targeting identification of 90% of GSE and interface issues in the laboratory before the first flight of any new configuration
* Engineer and validate mechanical and pneumatic launch system tests to ensure repeatable exit velocities and structural integrity, and maintain a ground-based Iron Bird environment in coordination with the IRB team to test system-wide reactions to simulated component failures
* Build and maintain a regression library of ground-side edge cases and failure scenarios that every new subsystem must clear before flight integration, achieving 100% coverage of critical failure modes such as GCS power failure and launcher misfire prior to any airworthy flight
* Set up and maintain HIL and SIL environments to simulate flight triggers on the bench, and perform vibration, thermal and EMI stress tests on supporting hardware to validate field survivability before deployment
* Generate technical de-risking reports and manage test cards in JIRA, maintaining a 48-hour bench-clearance turnaround for minor software or hardware patches and ensuring zero flight delays caused by preventable GSE failures

## **You should apply if you**

* Have designed and executed ground-side test programmes for UAV, aerospace or defence systems, with hands-on experience across Ground Control Stations, pneumatic or mechanical launch systems, and tracking antenna arrays
* Have built and maintained HIL and SIL environments and can sniff and analyse communication protocols between ground and air segments to de-risk command-and-control latency and packet loss
* Are proficient with diagnostic and instrumentation tools including oscilloscopes, spectrum analysers and network protocol analysers, and have performed root cause analysis on complex ground-side hardware failures in operationally relevant conditions
* Have conducted environmental and stress testing including vibration, thermal and EMI regimes on field-deployable hardware, with experience translating test findings into traceable de-risking reports and regression test assets
* Act decisively with minimal guidance, own outcomes end-to-end and initiate solutions without waiting for direction
