## **Who we are**

Helsing is a defence AI company. Our mission is to protect our democracies. We aim to achieve technological leadership, so that open societies can continue to make sovereign decisions and control their ethical standards. 

As democracies, we believe we have a special responsibility to be thoughtful about the development and deployment of powerful technologies like AI. We take this responsibility seriously. 

We are an ambitious and committed team of engineers, AI specialists and customer-facing programme managers. We are looking for mission-driven people to join our European teams – and apply their skills to solve the most complex and impactful problems. We embrace an open and transparent culture that welcomes healthy debates on the use of technology in defence, its benefits, and its ethical implications.

## **The role**

We are developing an advanced airborne platform with secure tactical communication and networking capabilities. We are looking for an experienced engineer to define and integrate tactical datalinks and airborne communication systems.

## The day-to-day

* Define tactical communications and datalink architectures.
* Integrate Link 16, MIDS/JTRS terminals and associated platform interfaces.
* Support integration of further tactical links such as Link 22, JREAP and VMF.
* Define requirements for antennas, radios, crypto equipment, mission computers and network-management functions.
* Perform link-budget, coverage, latency, throughput, interoperability and co-site interference analyses.
* Ensure compliance with applicable NATO standards and STANAGs.
* Support system integration, interoperability testing, ground testing and flight testing.

## **You should apply if you**

* Degree in communications engineering, electrical engineering or a related discipline.
* Several years of practical experience with military tactical communications or datalink systems.
* Proven experience with Link 16 and MIDS terminal integration is required.
* Strong knowledge of JTIDS/MIDS message exchange, network participation groups, time-slot allocation and tactical network design.
* Experience with relevant standards such as STANAG 5516, STANAG 5522, STANAG 5602 and MIL-STD-6016.
* Understanding of RF communications, waveforms, encryption, frequency hopping, anti-jam techniques and airborne antenna integration.
* Experience with system requirements, interface-control documents, qualification and interoperability testing.
