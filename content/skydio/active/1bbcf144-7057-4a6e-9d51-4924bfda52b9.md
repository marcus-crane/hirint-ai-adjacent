Skydio is the leading US drone company and the world leader in autonomous flight, the key technology for the future of drones and aerial mobility. The Skydio team combines deep expertise in artificial intelligence, best-in-class hardware and software product development, operational excellence, and customer obsession to empower a broader, more diverse audience of drone users, from [utility inspectors](https://www.skydio.com/solutions/energy-and-utilities) to [first responders](https://www.skydio.com/solutions/public-safety), [soldiers in battlefield scenarios](https://www.skydio.com/solutions/national-security/tactical-isr), and [beyond](https://www.skydio.com/solutions).

**About the role:** We are looking for a Lead Electrical Engineer (senior / staff) who will architect, develop, and refine the electrical systems for [Skydio F10 Program](https://www.skydio.com/f10), one of the world's highest performance fixed wing drone systems. This state-of-the-art program brings together computing hardware, sensing, and power to enable the next generation of autonomous drones.

**How you'll make an impact:**

* Drive architecture design for one of our newest systems
* Work cross functionally between EE, FW, RF, Reliability, and Mechanical teams
* Lead trade studies, understand competing design priorities, and provide direction and clarity for burning down risk
* Design and develop power electronics, controls, sensors, high speed communication
* Engineer electronics for volume production from product requirements to prototype to schematic capture to layout and validation
* Mentor and lead junior team members

**What makes you a good fit:**

* Comfortable making progress in open-ended problems that span multiple disciplines (ME, SW, Aero, Thermal, etc.)
* Enjoy working independently and owning designs
* Being able to communicate technical challenges to both fellow engineers and non-engineers alike
* Past success developing electronics for critical systems in aerospace or transportation
* Expertise with high and low speed protocols such as MIPI, Ethernet, CAN, RS-485, UART, I2C, SPI
* Experience with batteries and charging
* Familiarity with motors and motor drive
* Enthusiasm for working in a fast-paced, collaborative, environment to make novel products in a blossoming industry
* Obtaining FAA Part 107 certification within the first 60 days of employment is strongly encouraged for all Skydio employees and required for certain positions.
