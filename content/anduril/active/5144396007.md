Anduril Industries is a defense technology company with a mission to transform U.S. and allied military capabilities with advanced technology. By bringing the expertise, technology, and business model of the 21st century’s most innovative companies to the defense industry, Anduril is changing how military systems are designed, built and sold. Anduril’s family of systems is powered by Lattice OS, an AI-powered operating system that turns thousands of data streams into a realtime, 3D command and control center. As the world enters an era of strategic competition, Anduril is committed to bringing cutting-edge autonomy, AI, computer vision, sensor fusion, and networking technology to the military in months, not years.

Anduril is seeking a Senior Integration & Test Engineer to join the Omen team at our Costa Mesa HQ. Omen is Anduril’s hover-to-cruise autonomous air vehicle (AAV) that packs the endurance, payload, and mission flexibility of much larger airplanes into a novel runway-independent Group 3 platform. Omen can operate from ship decks, beaches, or remote island clearings, providing true runway independence and freedom of maneuver across defense and civilian domains. Omen is the first tail-sitter aircraft with a mass-production contract, with an order of 50 systems representing a decisive investment that anchors production and accelerates the development of future systems.  
  
As a Senior Integration & Test Engineer you will own the build and integration process. You are the bridge between engineering design and the technicians and mechanics executing the build, ensuring that every vehicle comes together on schedule, to spec, and ready for test. You will manage build packages, track material readiness, disposition discrepancies, and drive continuous improvement in how we turn engineering intent into flight-ready hardware.

## WHAT YOU'LL DO

* Own the vehicle build and integration process from BOM release through flight-ready handoff
* Review and validate build packages, work orders, and engineering drawings for completeness and producibility
* Ensure material readiness: track parts availability, coordinate procurement gaps, and manage kit staging
* Serve as the primary technical interface between design engineering and build technicians
* Identify, document, and disposition build discrepancies and non-conformances; drive root cause resolution
* Maintain and track build schedules across multiple vehicle builds and locations
* Manage configuration control for vehicle builds, ensuring as-built records match as-designed intent
* Support factory acceptance testing and first-power activities following integration
* Develop and refine integration procedures, acceptance criteria, and workmanship standards
* Participate in design reviews to provide integration and producibility feedback to engineering
* Travel to build locations to oversee integration activities, support technicians, and resolve issues in real-time
* Roll up your sleeves when needed — willing to get hands-on alongside the build team to keep things moving

## REQUIRED QUALIFICATIONS

* Bachelor's degree in Mechanical, Electrical, Aerospace, Systems, or Mechatronics Engineering
* 8+ years of experience in systems integration, vehicle build, or hardware integration and test
* Demonstrated experience managing or coordinating build/integration activities for complex electromechanical systems
* Proficient in reading and interpreting engineering drawings, schematics, and specifications
* Experience with configuration management and as-built documentation practices
* Familiarity with BOM structures, work order systems, and material planning
* Knowledge of electronics troubleshooting and ability to use basic test equipment
* Strong written and verbal communication skills, able to translate engineering intent into clear direction for build teams
* Self-motivated with high ownership; bias toward action when problems arise
* Ability to obtain and maintain a U.S. Secret security clearance
* Ability to travel approximately 50% of the time

## PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS

* Experience with rotorcraft or VTOL platforms
* Background in uncrewed or autonomous vehicle integration
* Experience with Group 3 or larger UAS programs
* Defense industry experience
* Experience developing or improving integration/manufacturing processes in a low-rate or prototype production environment
* Familiarity with data acquisition systems and instrumentation

### **Protecting Yourself from Recruitment Scams**

Anduril is committed to maintaining the integrity of our Talent acquisition process and the security of our candidates. * **Please always verify communications:**
  + Direct from Anduril: If you receive an email from one of our recruiters, it will *only* come from an `@anduril.com` address.
  + Via Agency Partner: If contacted by a recruiting agency for an Anduril role, their email will clearly identify their agency. If you suspect any suspicious activity, please verify the agency's authenticity by reaching out to [contact@anduril.com](mailto:contact@anduril.com).

* **What to Do If You Suspect Fraud:** Should you encounter any questionable or fraudulent outreach claiming to be from Anduril, please report it immediately to [contact@anduril.com](mailto:contact@anduril.com). Your proactive caution is invaluable in protecting your personal information and upholding the security and trustworthiness of our recruitment efforts.
