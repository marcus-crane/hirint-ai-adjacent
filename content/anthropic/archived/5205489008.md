## **About Anthropic**

Anthropic’s mission is to create reliable, interpretable, and steerable AI systems. We want AI to be safe and beneficial for our users and for society as a whole. Our team is a quickly growing group of committed researchers, engineers, policy experts, and business leaders working together to build beneficial AI systems.

## **About the role**

We are seeking a Product Finance, Inference Capacity Lead to join our Finance team at Anthropic. In this role, you will be a key partner to our product teams, engineering leaders, and go-to-market teams, providing financial expertise and guidance to support strategic decision-making. As we continue to grow, you will play a crucial role in analyzing financial data, building detailed financial models, and delivering insights to drive informed business decisions across our inference infrastructure—one of the biggest line items on our P&L.

This role will work closely with senior Compute, Product, and GTM stakeholders, and collaborating with other members of the finance organization to align product and inference strategies with broader financial considerations. You'll be passionate about optimizing and driving clarity into the impact that inference capacity has on our growth potential, while creating and scaling novel unit economic metrics for the business to operate by. If you are passionate about leveraging financial acumen to support innovation and drive business success at our cutting-edge AI company, join us on the frontier of making AI safe and impactful.

## **Key responsibilities**

* Provide expert guidance, recommendations, and influence to product, engineering, and go-to-market stakeholders on financial strategies, investments, and resource allocation decisions, leveraging an in-depth understanding of the company's financials
* Partner cross-functionally with senior engineering leaders and technical teams to drive detailed financial modeling and analysis, building sophisticated models to optimize inference infrastructure costs and performance
* Lead the creation and scaling of novel unit economic metrics for the business, including LTV, contribution margin, and other key performance indicators that enable data-driven decision-making
* Drive optimization initiatives and provide clarity into inference infrastructure costs, working to maximize efficiency of one of our largest P&L line items
* Partner cross-functionally to drive analysis, build financial models, and recommend business decisions across a variety of areas, such as pricing and packaging, business expansion, and defining new unit economic frameworks
* Prepare and deliver clear, concise, and insightful financial reports, analyses, and forecasts, highlighting key trends, risks, and opportunities to facilitate informed decision-making to stakeholders and executives
* Collaborate with other members of Finance & Strategy and accounting counterparts, helping to align product and inference strategies with broader financial considerations
* Establish and implement reporting dashboards and scorecards to track key operational and financial metrics

## **How we're different**

We believe that the highest-impact AI research will be big science. At Anthropic we work as a single cohesive team on just a few large-scale research efforts. And we value impact — advancing our long-term goals of steerable, trustworthy AI — rather than work on smaller and more specific puzzles. We view AI research as an empirical science, which has as much in common with physics and biology as with traditional efforts in computer science. We're an extremely collaborative group, and we host frequent research discussions to ensure that we are pursuing the highest-impact work at any given time. As such, we greatly value communication skills.

The easiest way to understand our research directions is to read our recent research. This research continues many of the directions our team worked on prior to Anthropic, including: GPT-3, Circuit-Based Interpretability, Multimodal Neurons, Scaling Laws, AI & Compute, Concrete Problems in AI Safety, and Learning from Human Preferences.
