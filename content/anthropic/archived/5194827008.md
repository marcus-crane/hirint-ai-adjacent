## **About Anthropic**

Anthropic’s mission is to create reliable, interpretable, and steerable AI systems. We want AI to be safe and beneficial for our users and for society as a whole. Our team is a quickly growing group of committed researchers, engineers, policy experts, and business leaders working together to build beneficial AI systems.

## **About the Role**

Your primary mission is network operations: deploying and maintaining network infrastructure, overseeing smarthands vendors, and owning the physical infrastructure lifecycle from install through decommission. You’ll also flex into other site work as the network infrastructure team needs you.

This isn’t a maintenance gig; you’ll be building the processes, playbooks, and operational discipline that the team scales on. You’ll work across sites, travel regularly, and have a direct impact on the infrastructure that powers Claude.

## **Responsibilities**

* Own network infrastructure deployment, maintenance, and bringup across sites
* Oversee outside plant and long-haul fiber optic repair vendors: manage dispatches, validate work quality, and ensure SLA compliance
* Execute network device installation, configuration validation, and commissioning support
* Own RMA processes end-to-end: triage, swap, ship, track, close
* Manage spares inventory and coordinate logistics across sites
* Conduct physical asset auditing and cradle-to-grave lifecycle tracking
* Coordinate with vendors for outsourced work and hold them accountable
* Build repeatable processes and playbooks that scale with the team
* Support various site deployments like: rack-and-stack, commissioning, forklift, decommissioning as needed
* Travel up to 40 - 60% to sites across the U.S.

## **You may be a good fit if you**

* Have 5+ years (3+ considered for the right candidate) of hands-on experience in field engineering, network operations, or telecom infrastructure
* Understand fiber optic systems well enough to oversee vendor work: you can validate splicing, testing, and repair quality without necessarily doing it yourself
* Are familiar with network hardware deployment (switches, routers, optics)
* Have managed RMA workflows and spares logistics across multiple sites
* Hold relevant certifications or are willing to obtain them (OSHA 10/30, BICSI, fiber certifications, confined space)
* Can work independently in the field with minimal supervision: you know how to figure it out, and are not afraid to reach out and ask questions when needed
* Have experience building processes from scratch, not just following them
* Thrive in ambiguity and move fast without waiting for perfect information
* Have a bias toward action: you’d rather do than talk about doing
* Communicate clearly and directly, especially when flagging risks or blockers

## **How we're different**

We believe that the highest-impact AI research will be big science. At Anthropic we work as a single cohesive team on just a few large-scale research efforts. And we value impact — advancing our long-term goals of steerable, trustworthy AI — rather than work on smaller and more specific puzzles. We view AI research as an empirical science, which has as much in common with physics and biology as with traditional efforts in computer science. We're an extremely collaborative group, and we host frequent research discussions to ensure that we are pursuing the highest-impact work at any given time. As such, we greatly value communication skills.

The easiest way to understand our research directions is to read our recent research. This research continues many of the directions our team worked on prior to Anthropic, including: GPT-3, Circuit-Based Interpretability, Multimodal Neurons, Scaling Laws, AI & Compute, Concrete Problems in AI Safety, and Learning from Human Preferences.
