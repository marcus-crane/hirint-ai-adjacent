## **About Anthropic**

Anthropic’s mission is to create reliable, interpretable, and steerable AI systems. We want AI to be safe and beneficial for our users and for society as a whole. Our team is a quickly growing group of committed researchers, engineers, policy experts, and business leaders working together to build beneficial AI systems.

As the Life Sciences Industry Advisor at Anthropic, you will serve as the technical and scientific face of Claude to the world's leading pharmaceutical companies, biotechs, contract research organizations, and research institutions. You'll combine deep life sciences domain expertise with technical credibility to help customers envision, architect, and realize transformational AI solutions across the drug discovery and development lifecycle — while building Anthropic's reputation as the trusted AI platform for one of the most rigorous and regulated industries in the world.

This role sits at the intersection of scientific leadership, customer engagement, product strategy, and internal enablement. You'll work directly with Chief Scientific Officers, Chief Medical Officers, Heads of R&D, Chief Digital & Data Officers, Heads of Regulatory Affairs, and computational and translational science leaders at major biopharma and research organizations to design solutions that address their most critical challenges — from literature synthesis and hypothesis generation to bioinformatics, clinical trial design, and regulatory submission. Your insights will flow directly to Product and Research, shaping Claude's roadmap for life sciences. Equally important, you'll enable Anthropic's GTM teams with deep domain expertise, thought leadership content, and strategic guidance.

With AI poised to compress drug development timelines and the industry seeking partners who understand the science, the workflows, and the regulatory bar, you'll play a pivotal role in establishing Anthropic as the AI platform of choice for life sciences worldwide.

## **Key responsibilities**

* **Technical Evangelism & Thought Leadership:** Represent Anthropic's scientific and technical vision to the life sciences industry through conference keynotes (e.g., J.P. Morgan Healthcare, BIO, DIA, SLAS, Bio-IT World), executive briefings, whitepapers, and scientific advisory boards. Establish Claude as the leading AI platform across discovery research, translational and clinical development, and regulatory affairs.
* **Strategic Customer Engagement:** Serve as executive technical sponsor for strategic life sciences accounts, engaging CSOs, CMOs, Heads of R&D, Chief Digital/Data Officers, and Heads of AI/Computational Science on enterprise AI strategy. Build peer-to-peer relationships that position Anthropic as a strategic R&D transformation partner.
* **Product Partnership & Roadmap Influence:** Translate customer requirements and competitive dynamics into product priorities. Partner closely with Product and Research to develop life-sciences-specific capabilities including scientific accuracy and citation integrity, GxP/validated workflows, data privacy and security, model governance, explainability, and auditability.
* **Pricing & Commercial Strategy:** Contribute to pricing and packaging decisions with life sciences market insight. Understand value drivers across discovery, development, and commercial functions, competitive positioning, and deal structuring that aligns with how biopharma and research organizations buy technology.
* **Internal Enablement & Advocacy:** Train and uplift Anthropic's GTM teams on life sciences domain expertise, scientific workflows, use cases, and buyer personas. Create enablement content, conduct training sessions, and serve as the internal authority on all things life sciences. Influence without authority across the organization.
* **Regulatory & Compliance Navigation:** Partner internally and externally to develop thought leadership on AI governance frameworks aligned with FDA and EMA expectations, GxP and GAMP 5, 21 CFR Part 11, data integrity (ALCOA+), and patient-data privacy (HIPAA/GDPR). Help customers understand model risk management, validation, explainability requirements, and responsible AI practices for regulated scientific and clinical work.
* **Ecosystem Partnership:** Build technical relationships with GSIs and life sciences consultancies (Deloitte, Accenture, PwC, KPMG, ZS, IQVIA), cloud providers (AWS, Google Cloud, Azure), and life sciences platform/data vendors (Veeva, Benchling, Schrödinger, Medidata, 10x Genomics).

## **Minimum qualifications**

* Have 20+ years of experience in scientific, technology, and/or business roles with at least 8 years in senior leadership positions (CSO, CMO, VP/Head of R&D, Chief Data/Digital Officer, Head of Computational Sciences, Head of Regulatory Affairs, Distinguished Scientist/Engineer) in pharma, biotech, or a life sciences research organization
* Have deep domain expertise across one or more stages of the drug discovery and development lifecycle — discovery/translational research, bioinformatics/computational biology, clinical development, or regulatory affairs — with hands-on experience building or operating mission-critical scientific or development systems
* Have established executive networks across life sciences R&D and technology leadership (CSOs, CMOs, Heads of R&D / Data Science) and credibility as a thought leader in the industry
* Have deep knowledge of life sciences regulatory and quality requirements (FDA/EMA guidance, GxP, GAMP 5, 21 CFR Part 11, data integrity, computer system validation) and experience implementing compliant technology solutions
* Have experience influencing product roadmaps based on customer feedback and market requirements, working effectively with Product and Engineering teams

## **How we're different**

We believe that the highest-impact AI research will be big science. At Anthropic we work as a single cohesive team on just a few large-scale research efforts. And we value impact — advancing our long-term goals of steerable, trustworthy AI — rather than work on smaller and more specific puzzles. We view AI research as an empirical science, which has as much in common with physics and biology as with traditional efforts in computer science. We're an extremely collaborative group, and we host frequent research discussions to ensure that we are pursuing the highest-impact work at any given time. As such, we greatly value communication skills.

The easiest way to understand our research directions is to read our recent research. This research continues many of the directions our team worked on prior to Anthropic, including: GPT-3, Circuit-Based Interpretability, Multimodal Neurons, Scaling Laws, AI & Compute, Concrete Problems in AI Safety, and Learning from Human Preferences.
