## **About the Role**

We are looking for a Principal Data Center Architect with deep expertise in Civil, Structural, and Architectural (CSA) design to join our growing infrastructure team. This role is the technical owner for the physical building fabric of Anthropic's AI compute facilities — from the earliest site feasibility studies and test fits through reference design development, design partner oversight, and construction delivery.

As Anthropic scales its data center program across multiple sites in the United States and internationally, you will set the civil, structural, and architectural standards that govern how our facilities are designed and built. You will work directly with design-build partners, AE firms, and internal mechanical, electrical, and IT infrastructure teams to ensure our facilities are optimized for high-density AI workloads, constructable on aggressive timelines, and replicable across a growing portfolio.

This is an owner's engineer role — you are the technical authority, not a consultant. You will be expected to make binding design decisions, identify problems before they reach the field, and drive standardization across a pipeline of concurrent projects globally.

## **What You'll Do**

* Own the civil, structural, and architectural reference design standards for Anthropic's data center portfolio, establishing repeatable design elements deployable across multiple sites and jurisdictions
* Lead front-end test fits and site feasibility studies, evaluating structural approaches, site constraints, utility interfaces, and code compliance to inform site selection and design direction
* Review and approve CSA deliverables from design-build partners and AE firms at each design stage (SD, DD, CD, IFC), including drawings, structural calculations, specifications, and geotechnical reports
* Integrate CSA design with mechanical, electrical, plumbing, fire protection, and IT infrastructure teams to produce coordinated designs that support high-density AI compute and direct liquid-cooling systems
* Drive site and campus planning to maximize compute density, optimize construction phasing, and align physical layout with IT and operational requirements
* Partner with the construction and delivery team to provide technical oversight during construction, resolve field issues, and support commissioning activities
* Evaluate and develop relationships with design partners, structural engineering subconsultants, and prefabrication / modular vendors; define qualification and performance standards
* Establish and maintain facility support area standards — logistics staging, loading, maintenance access, and operational support spaces — scaled for hyperscale deployments
* Identify modular and prefabricated construction opportunities that reduce schedule, cost, and design risk across the project pipeline
* Manage review cycles and coordinate across internal and external stakeholders to resolve design conflicts and keep projects moving on aggressive timelines
* Travel to project sites globally for design reviews, construction audits, and commissioning milestones (up to 40%)

## **What We're Looking For**

**Required**

* 10+ years delivering mission-critical facility infrastructure, with at least 6 years directly in hyperscale or large-scale data center CSA design
* Deep technical expertise across civil site development, structural design, and architectural programming for industrial or mission-critical facilities
* Demonstrated experience as an owner's engineer or lead technical authority — setting standards, approving deliverables, and managing external design teams
* Proficiency with applicable building codes and standards including IBC, IFC, ASCE 7, ACI 318, AISC, FM Global, and local jurisdictional requirements across multiple geographies
* Experience with high-density compute environments, including structural and architectural accommodation of direct liquid-cooling systems, overhead busway, and dense rack deployments
* Track record of driving design standardization and developing repeatable reference designs across a multi-site portfolio
* Ability to evaluate and red-line structural calculations, civil site plans, and architectural construction documents for accuracy, completeness, and constructability
* Experience coordinating across MEP disciplines and IT infrastructure teams in a fast-paced, multi-project environment
* Clear and direct communication with both technical and non-technical audiences; ability to make and defend design decisions with incomplete information

**Preferred**

* Licensed Professional Engineer (PE) in civil or structural engineering, or licensed architect (AIA / RA)
* Experience with prefabricated and modular construction strategies for data center applications
* Familiarity with geotechnical risk assessment and foundation design for large industrial facilities
* LEED accreditation, Uptime Institute AOS/M&O certification, or equivalent mission-critical credentials
* International project delivery experience (Europe, Asia-Pacific, or other regions)
* Experience reviewing design packages in Autodesk Construction Cloud, Revit, or Navisworks

## **How we're different**

We believe that the highest-impact AI research will be big science. At Anthropic we work as a single cohesive team on just a few large-scale research efforts. And we value impact — advancing our long-term goals of steerable, trustworthy AI — rather than work on smaller and more specific puzzles. We view AI research as an empirical science, which has as much in common with physics and biology as with traditional efforts in computer science. We're an extremely collaborative group, and we host frequent research discussions to ensure that we are pursuing the highest-impact work at any given time. As such, we greatly value communication skills.

The easiest way to understand our research directions is to read our recent research. This research continues many of the directions our team worked on prior to Anthropic, including: GPT-3, Circuit-Based Interpretability, Multimodal Neurons, Scaling Laws, AI & Compute, Concrete Problems in AI Safety, and Learning from Human Preferences.
