## **About Anthropic**

Anthropic’s mission is to create reliable, interpretable, and steerable AI systems. We want AI to be safe and beneficial for our users and for society as a whole. Our team is a quickly growing group of committed researchers, engineers, policy experts, and business leaders working together to build beneficial AI systems.

## **About the role**

The web operations team is part of Anthropic's Creative Studio — the in-house team responsible for brand expression across Anthropic and Claude. We're responsible for how content gets built, published, and maintained across anthropic.com and claude.com, and we care deeply about quality, consistency, and getting things right.

We're looking for a Web Producer to join us in a hybrid production and content role, working at the intersection of editorial precision, web craft, and operational coordination. You'll be a trusted partner to content teams across the company, helping them navigate the publishing process, execute content updates, and build out pages that meet our quality bar. You'll also play a key role in keeping our web operations running smoothly by triaging incoming requests, coordinating smaller projects, and supporting launches with careful QA. You won't just execute tasks — you'll help improve the publishing experience for everyone who depends on it.

## **Key responsibilities**

* Build and publish content across our CMS platforms, including assembling templated pages and maintaining content collections with accuracy and consistency
* Own the web intake queue by triaging requests from partners, setting clear expectations on scope and timing, and keeping work moving
* Perform thorough QA across staging and production environments before content goes live, catching errors in copy, formatting, links, and rendering
* Support web launches and campaigns by coordinating content publishing across relevant surfaces and flagging risks before they become problems
* Partner with marketers and content teams to help them work effectively within our web infrastructure — answering questions, providing guidance, and reducing friction in their publishing experience
* Develop and maintain documentation and training resources that help partners publish confidently and independently
* Collaborate with design engineers and the broader web team to ensure content is implemented correctly and templates are used as intended
* Maintain the governance and training around publishing access and guidelines
* Identify patterns in recurring requests or issues and surface opportunities to improve how we work

## **Minimum qualifications**

* Have hands-on experience publishing and building content in a CMS
* Have strong attention to detail — you catch what others miss and hold a high quality bar for anything that goes live
* Have experience coordinating requests or projects across multiple stakeholders with competing priorities
* Are a clear communicator who can set expectations, provide updates, and explain technical concepts to non-technical partners
* Can document processes and create training materials that help others work more independently

## **Preferred qualifications**

* Experience working with headless CMS platforms (contentful, sanity etc…) and component-based design systems
* Background in web production, content operations, or digital publishing at a technology company or agency
* Experience building pages using structured templates
* Familiarity with basic HTML/CSS sufficient to identify and communicate rendering issues to engineering and design partners
* Comfort using project management or ticketing tools (such as Asana, Linear, or Jira) to manage and prioritize a request queue
* Understanding of SEO best practices as they apply to content structure, metadata, and page assembly
* Experience contributing to or owning documentation, onboarding resources, or internal knowledge bases

## **How we're different**

We believe that the highest-impact AI research will be big science. At Anthropic we work as a single cohesive team on just a few large-scale research efforts. And we value impact — advancing our long-term goals of steerable, trustworthy AI — rather than work on smaller and more specific puzzles. We view AI research as an empirical science, which has as much in common with physics and biology as with traditional efforts in computer science. We're an extremely collaborative group, and we host frequent research discussions to ensure that we are pursuing the highest-impact work at any given time. As such, we greatly value communication skills.

The easiest way to understand our research directions is to read our recent research. This research continues many of the directions our team worked on prior to Anthropic, including: GPT-3, Circuit-Based Interpretability, Multimodal Neurons, Scaling Laws, AI & Compute, Concrete Problems in AI Safety, and Learning from Human Preferences.
