**Job Description:**

As a Senior Staff Systems Engineer on Shield AI’s next gen UAS program, you will play a pivotal role in designing, developing, and integrating cutting-edge autonomous aircraft systems for national defense. You will lead the technical execution of system architecture, ensuring seamless integration of avionics, autonomy, propulsion, sensor payloads, and mission-critical systems.

This position requires a deep understanding of aerospace systems engineering, autonomy, and complex system integration. You will work cross-functionally with teams in hardware, software, autonomy, flight operations, and manufacturing to develop a scalable, mission-ready platform capable of operating in contested environments.

Your expertise will be critical in defining system requirements and architectures through model-based systems engineering, conducting trade studies, ensuring compliance with military and aviation standards, and supporting the aircraft through flight testing and operational deployment.

### What You'll Do:

* Perform System Architecture & Design: Develop and refine the system architecture for the next gen UAS, ensuring seamless integration of avionics, autonomy, propulsion, sensor payloads, and mission systems.
* Define and document **system-level behavior**, including modes, states, transitions, and fault responses
* Support safety analyses (FHA, FMEA, FTA) by defining behavior under fault conditions
* Define and Manage System Requirements: Establish top-level requirements, decompose them into subsystems, and maintain traceability across the development lifecycle.
* Autonomy & AI Integration: Work closely with autonomy and software teams to integrate AI-driven flight control, decision-making, and mission execution capabilities.
* Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE): Utilize SysML, DOORS, or other MBSE tools to create digital system models, conduct trade studies, and optimize system performance.
* Risk Management & Trade Studies: Conduct trade studies to evaluate design alternatives, assess technical risks, and ensure the best engineering solutions for mission effectiveness.
* Stakeholder & Customer Engagement: Work closely with DoD stakeholders, industry partners, and government agencies to align system capabilities with operational requirements.
* Cross-Functional Collaboration: Partner with hardware, autonomy, software, propulsion, and flight operations teams to drive seamless system integration and rapid development cycles.

### Required Qualifications:

* 10+ years of experience in systems engineering for aerospace, defense, or autonomous aircraft systems.
* Demonstrated experience defining system behavior logic and operational modes
* Deep expertise in system architecture, avionics integration, and autonomy-driven flight systems.
* Proficiency in MBSE, DOORS, SysML, CAMEO, or similar system modeling tools for complex systems design and analysis.
* Experience with Group 4 or Group 5 UAS, military aircraft, or autonomous airborne platforms.
* Strong background in flight testing, system integration, and performance validation.
* Knowledge of DoD acquisition processes, MIL-STDs, FAA airworthiness certification, and airborne system safety standards.
* Demonstrated ability to lead cross-functional teams and drive complex projects to successful completion.
* Ability to work in a fast-paced, agile development environment with shifting priorities.
* Worked on developmental program from clean sheet to first flight
* Ability to obtain a S//SAR level security clearance desired.

### Preferred Qualifications:

* Experience developing AI-driven autonomy for unmanned aircraft.
* Expertise in ISR, EW, and advanced sensor integration for airborne platforms.
* Familiarity with modular open system architectures (MOSA) and Government Reference Architectures (GRA) for defense applications.
* Prior experience working with defense primes, DoD stakeholders, or government R&D programs.
* Active Secret or Top Secret clearance (or ability to obtain one).
