### Job Description:

Shield AI is seeking a Actuated and Fluid Systems Engineer to support development of hydraulic and actuated systems for our groundbreaking jet-engine VTOL military UAV platform, X-BAT. You will design components and fluid systems, engage with suppliers, perform system and component analysis and sizing, and own multiple aspects of X-BAT’s hydraulics and actuation systems.

### What you'll do:

* Work with the engineering team to own design, analysis, and implementation of hydraulic and actuated system designs
* Engage with and manage suppliers to establish component and system specifications, purchase COTS equipment, and support on-time delivery of your system(s) to the aircraft.
* Design and execute tests to ensure component and system-level performance meets requirements
* Own and manage lightweight system level requirements
* Work with cross-functional stakeholders across the actuated systems group and the aircraft to ensure excellent implementation of your designs

### Required qualifications:

* B.S. in Mechanical Engineering (or equivalent).
* 5+ years experience with fluid or hydraulic system design, component design, system analysis, supplier management, requirements management, etc.
* Working knowledge of hydraulic components (relief valves, pumps, reservoirs, accumulators, actuators, fluid control valves)
* Demonstrated ability to execute design, build, test with rigor at speed
* Strong CAD skills (NX preferred)
* Demonstrated proficiency with hydraulic system analysis, including 1st principles, Simscape, Amesim, etc.
* Ability to work independently in fast-paced, cross-functional teams
* A demonstrated record of working hard, being trustworthy, holding yourself to high standards, and being kind to others

### Preferred qualifications:

* Experience with environmental and operational robustness (vibration/shock, thermal extremes, contamination, corrosion, sand/dust, etc.).
* Experience supporting ground test and flight test campaigns and system checkout, failure investigations.
* Familiarity with reliability and safety methods (DFMEA/FTA, fault containment, safe-state thinking) in collaboration with systems safety.
