**About the Team**

The Physical Security Engineering team designs, deploys, and sustains the systems that protect OpenAI’s people, facilities, and critical infrastructure. We work across global office campuses, warehouses, labs and data centers, partnering with IT, Facilities, Construction, and Security Operations to ensure all environments meet stringent safety, reliability, and compliance requirements.

Our team owns security architecture from early site due-diligence through construction, commissioning, and long-term lifecycle management.

**About the Role**

OpenAI’s physical security technology environment is growing quickly, and the Corporate Security Technology Group needs a hands-on systems engineer who can keep the stack stable, responsive, and scalable day to day. This role is a hybrid of physical security infrastructure engineering, operational systems support, stakeholder coordination, and vendor management. It should not be framed as a badge or camera administrator role.

This person will support the systems that power OpenAI’s access control, video surveillance, GSOC workflows, and physical security operations. They will troubleshoot technical issues, manage operational tickets and escalations, coordinate remediation with integrators, support system programming and audits, and partner closely with IT/NetEng and Workplace to keep systems reliable as OpenAI expands.

**In this role, you will**

* Troubleshoot complex physical security system issues across access control, video management, GSOC workflows, and supporting infrastructure.
* Manage operational tickets and escalations with strong follow-through, documentation, and root-cause thinking.
* Coordinate service requests, remediation work, and quality control with security integrators and vendors.
* Support GSOC workflows, including system access management, auditing, feature requests, and security systems programming.
* Partner with networking and infrastructure teams on VLANs, firewall policies, IP schemas, switch configurations, IoT device connectivity, Windows Server, and database dependencies.
* Improve operational consistency across offices by reducing manual work, documenting repeatable processes, and identifying automation opportunities.

**What’s success?**

Security Operations, GSOC, Workplace, IT, and CorpSec partners can rely on stable, accurate, and well-supported physical security systems. Issues are triaged quickly, escalations have clear ownership, vendors receive precise direction, and recurring problems turn into better documentation, process, or automation. The hire helps the team support rapid office growth without letting reliability or operational quality drift.

**Non-negotiable outcomes**

* Physical security systems issues are handled with clear triage, strong technical reasoning, and complete follow-through.
* GSOC and Security Operations can depend on accurate access management, auditing, system programming, and support workflows.
* Integrator and vendor work is coordinated tightly, with clear accountability and reduced back-and-forth.
* Networking, server, database, and device dependencies are understood well enough to diagnose issues across the full stack.
* Operational toil and vendor dependency decrease through better documentation, process improvements, and automation opportunities.
