## **About the Role**

Cognition is building its federal business, and capture is the engine that drives it. We're hiring a Capture Lead to partner directly with Federal leadership and own that engine end to end—from identifying the right pursuits to standing up the systems that make winning repeatable. This is a builder's role: you'll create the capture function, not inherit one.

**What you'll do**

*Capture strategy & planning*

* Partner with Federal sales leadership on quarterly and annual capture planning
* Partner with GTM, Mission Success, and Engineering on deal structuring, pricing strategy, and negotiation across contract vehicles
* Track and prioritize contract vehicle strategy (GSA, OTAs, IDIQs, SBIR pathways, prime/sub relationships)

*Pursuit & proposal operations*

* Identify and track relevant federal solicitations, from initial RFI through final RFP
* Build the operational backbone for proposal response: intake, qualification, bid/no-bid decisioning, color team review cadence, and compliance
* Manage relationships with proposal and capture partners supporting RFI-to-RFP response on Cognition's behalf
* Build and maintain proposal collateral, and maintain the past performance, capabilities, and pricing libraries that compound across pursuits

*GTM systems & performance*

* Define the metrics that matter for a federal GTM motion and build dashboards that surface them
* Own solicitation response tracking across active pursuits
* Run the red-team/AAR process on wins and losses; turn lessons learned into repeatable best practices

*Cross-functional execution*

* Identify operational bottlenecks and build the systems, processes, and tooling to remove them

**What we're looking for**

* 4+ years of experience in federal business operations, capture management, or revenue strategy at a defense tech, federal SaaS, or government services company
* Deep fluency in federal procurement: contract vehicles, working knowledge of FAR/DFARS, capture lifecycle, and the rhythm of federal sales cycles
* Strong analytical foundation—you can build the model, run the analysis, and tell the story
* Track record of working in and building capture programs from scratch in a fast-moving environment
* Comfort operating without a playbook, and a low ego about doing the work yourself
* Based in Washington, DC, or willing to relocate
* Security clearance preferred, or ability to obtain one

**What success looks like in the first year**

* A functioning bid/no-bid and proposal process with clear ownership
* Live pipeline visibility and win/loss reporting leadership actually uses
* A measurable improvement in win rate or pursuit efficiency
