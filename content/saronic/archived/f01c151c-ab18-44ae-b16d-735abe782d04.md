Saronic Technologies is a leader in revolutionizing autonomy at sea, dedicated to developing state-of-the-art solutions that enhance maritime operations through autonomous and intelligent platforms.

# **Job Overview**

Responsible for coordinating and overseeing maintenance and repairs across all company vehicles, golf carts, forklifts, and heavy/specialized equipment. Serves as the primary point of contact between internal stakeholders and external vendors to ensure equipment is safe, compliant, and operational

# Responsibilities

* Manage preventive and reactive maintenance for all fleet vehicles, golf carts, forklifts, and heavy/miscellaneous equipment
* Coordinate vendor scheduling, onsite management, and service follow-through
* Partner with the DOT Training Manager and Fleet Senior Manager on documentation, compliance tracking, and recordkeeping
* Maintain accurate maintenance logs, work orders, and repair histories
* Source and manage vendor relationships; onboard new vendors as equipment needs arise
* Identify and escalate equipment concerns, safety issues, or licensing requirements to appropriate teams (EHS, HR, etc.)
* Assist with rental equipment management
* Monitor ticketing system for maintenance/repair requests or failed safety Pre/Post Trip inspections.
* Collaborate with departments on NEW equipment request sourcing and work with Finance/AP for guidance on purchase vs. Rent decisions.
* Analyze current equipment rentals across the U.S for deficiencies and savings related to Purchase vs. Rent

# **Qualifications**

* Experience in fleet, facilities, or equipment maintenance coordination
* Familiarity with DOT regulations and compliance requirements. (Not Required but could be beneficial)
* Strong organizational and communication skills
* Ability to manage multiple vendors and equipment types simultaneously
* Background in cost saving initiatives related to maintenance plans, or creating/improving process.

# **Physical Demands**

* Prolonged periods of sitting at a desk and working on a computer
* Occasional standing and walking within the office
* Manual dexterity to operate a computer keyboard, mouse, and other office equipment
* Visual acuity to read screens, documents, and reports
* Occasional reaching, bending, or stooping to access file drawers, cabinets, or office supplies
* Lifting and carrying items up to 20 pounds occasionally (e.g., office supplies, packages)
