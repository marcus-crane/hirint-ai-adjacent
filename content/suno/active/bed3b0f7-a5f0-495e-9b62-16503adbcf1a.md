# **About the Role**

As a Software Engineer in the Data Platform team at Suno, you will help build the systems and practices that ensure our data is secure, well-managed, and trustworthy as we scale. This role sits at the intersection of Data, Engineering, Security, and Legal, and plays an important part in helping Suno operate as a mature, audit-ready organization.

As we grow, we need increasing clarity around questions like *“Who owns this data?”*, *“Who has access to it and why?”*, and *“How was this metric produced?”*. You’ll support the development of governance frameworks that make these answers clear and consistent, while helping teams adopt best practices around data access, documentation, and usage.

This is a hands-on role where you’ll work closely with more senior members of the Data Platform team to implement governance controls, improve documentation, and support audit readiness. It’s a great opportunity for someone who wants to deepen their experience in data governance, security, and compliance in a fast-moving environment.

Check out the Suno version of this role here: <https://suno.com/s/UngzQHqVgblYBTKn>

# **What You’ll Do**

* Support the implementation of data governance frameworks that improve data ownership, access, and accountability across the organization.
* Help manage role-based access control (RBAC) in Snowflake, including provisioning access, reviewing permissions, and ensuring adherence to least-privilege principles.
* Assist in defining and maintaining data policies around access, usage, and retention, in collaboration with Data, Security, and Legal teams.
* Develop and optimize data models and transformations in Snowflake using DBT, ensuring data is clean, well-structured, and analytics-ready.
* Build tooling and abstractions that make it easy for other teams to produce and consume high-quality data.
* Maintain and improve documentation, including data definitions, ownership records, and governance standards.
* Contribute to building and maintaining data lineage and metadata tracking, helping improve visibility into how data flows across systems.
* Support audit readiness by helping collect evidence, maintain access logs, and participate in periodic access reviews.
* Monitor data access patterns and flag potential issues, inconsistencies, or policy gaps.
* Partner with Engineering and Analytics teams to ensure governance practices are incorporated into data pipelines and workflows.
* Help improve data quality and consistency by identifying gaps in definitions, ownership, or validation processes.
* Promote good data hygiene and responsible data usage across teams through documentation and collaboration.

# **What You’ll Need**

* 3–5 years of experience in Data, Analytics, Data Engineering, or related roles, with some exposure to data governance, data quality, or data security concepts.
* Working knowledge of SQL and familiarity with querying and exploring data in modern data warehouses (Snowflake preferred).
* Basic understanding of access controls, RBAC, and data security best practices.
* Familiarity with modern data tools (e.g., dbt, Airflow, or similar), and how data pipelines are structured.
* Experience or exposure to infrastructure as code (e.g., Terraform) for managing data platform resources such as Snowflake roles, warehouses, and permissions.
* Interest in data governance, compliance, and auditability, with a desire to grow deeper in this area.
* Strong attention to detail and an appreciation for clean, well-documented systems.
* Ability to work cross-functionally and communicate clearly with both technical and non-technical stakeholders.
* A pragmatic mindset — able to balance structure with speed in a startup environment.
* Strong sense of ownership and willingness to learn in a fast-moving, evolving environment.
* A genuine passion for music, creativity, and AI, and excitement about supporting a trustworthy data foundation for generative creation.
