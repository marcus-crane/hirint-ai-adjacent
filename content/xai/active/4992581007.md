### ABOUT THE ROLE:

The O&M Technician is responsible for the safe, reliable, and efficient operation and maintenance of a 1.2 GW behind-the-meter simple-cycle load following facility consisting of multiple LM6000 aeroderivative gas turbine generators, balance-of-plant systems, and associated electrical distribution.

This is a true multi-skilled “hybrid” position requiring proven hands-on expertise in control room operations, mechanical maintenance, electrical, instrumentation & controls (I&C), and water treatment/chemistry for the LM6000 platform.

### RESPONSIBILITIES:

* Perform Control Room Operator duties:
  + Safely start, operate, monitor, and shut down LM6000 gas turbines and BOP systems.
  + Execute fast-start dispatches, respond to alarms, and provide effective communication to the facility operations team.
  + Maintain shift logs, LOTO records, and regulatory compliance documentation
* Perform Mechanical maintenance specific to LM6000:
  + Perform Level 1–2 maintenance, borescope inspections, combustor/TBC inspections, and unit alignment.
  + Replace VBV/IGV actuators, perform lube oil system maintenance, and service starters/clutches
* Perform Electrical maintenance:
  + Test and maintain 13.8 kV switchgear, generator breakers, and MODs
  + Test exciter, and AVR; calibrate protective relays; support black-start diesel
  + Troubleshoot 480 V MCCs, test motors, and perform infrared thermography
* Perform Instrumentation & Controls (I&C) duties:
  + Calibrate and troubleshoot vibration, temperature, pressure, and speed sensors
  + Test fire & gas systems (Det-Tronics, etc.) and perform loop checks
* Perform Water Treatment & Chemistry duties:
  + OperateReverse Osmosis/demineralized water system daily for NOx water, SPRINT, fogging, and water wash system.
  + Test water purity (conductivity, silica, TDS, ph), dose chemicals, and regenerate resin beds
* Execute LOTO, hot work, confined-space permits
* Lead or support scheduled LM6000 borescope, combustion, and hot section outages
* Maintain critical spares inventory and CMMS records.
* Mentor apprentices and contractors

### BASIC QUALIFICATIONS:

* Minimum 2–5 years of direct hands-on experience on LM 6000 aeroderivative turbines (Level 1/2/3 maintenance qualified strongly preferred)
* Demonstrated competency in at least three of the five core disciplines: Control Room Operations, Mechanical, Electrical, I&C, and Water Treatment/Chemistry
* Ability to read and interpret LM6000-specific P&IDs, electrical one-line diagrams, Medium-voltage experience is a plus
* Strong computer skills
* Valid driver’s license and ability to pass background check, drug screen, respiratory fit test, and physical
* Physical Requirements:  
  + Work at heights, in confined spaces, and around high-voltage equipment
  + Lift 50+ lbs repeatedly, climb turbine enclosures, and wear full PPE including respirator
  + Tolerate high heat and humidity inside LM6000 enclosures and outdoor extreme weather conditions

### PREFERRED SKILLS AND EXPERIENCE:

* LM6000 Level 1, 2, or 3 Maintenance Certification
* DCS Maintenance & Troubleshooting
* NFPA 70E Electrical Safety
* Vibration Analyst Category II or higher
